Welcome to Watany Eye Hospitals, where we specialize in cutting-edge eye care procedures, including Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) implantation surgery. Below is a detailed overview of ICL implantation surgery, including types, precautions, and what to expect before, during, and after the procedure.

Introduction to ICL Implantation Surgery

• ICL implantation surgery offers a revolutionary solution for individuals seeking freedom from glasses or contact lenses.

• Unlike traditional LASIK or PRK surgeries, which reshape the cornea, ICL surgery involves the insertion of a small, flexible lens inside the eye to correct refractive errors such as myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism.

• This procedure is particularly suitable for patients with high refractive errors or thinner corneas.

What are the benefits of ICL surgery?

• Corrects a wider range of vision problems than LASIK, including high levels of nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism.

• May be a suitable option for people with thin corneas who are not candidates for LASIK.

• Offers a permanent vision correction solution.

• Results in clearer, sharper vision.

Types of ICLs

At Watany Eye Hospitals, we offer various types of ICLs to cater to the unique needs of each patient. These include:

Visian ICL: Made of Collamer, a biocompatible material, Visian ICLs are known for their flexibility and optical clarity.

Visian Toric ICL: Specifically designed for patients with astigmatism, Visian Toric ICLs correct both spherical and cylindrical refractive errors, providing excellent visual outcomes.

Preconceptions for ICL Surgery

Before undergoing ICL surgery, you will need to undergo a comprehensive eye examination to determine if you are a good candidate for the procedure. This examination will include:

• Complete review of your medical history.

• Measurement of your corneal thickness.

• Measurement of your pupil size.

• Mapping of the curvature of your cornea (topography).

• Tonometry test to measure your eye pressure.

before, during, and after ICL surgery

•You will need to stop wearing contact lenses for a few weeks before surgery. You may also need to avoid certain medications that can thin your blood.

•Local anesthesia will be administered to numb the eye, ensuring you remain comfortable throughout the procedure. The surgeon will make a small incision in your cornea and fold the ICL lens. The lens is then inserted into your eye and unfolded into position behind your iris. ICL implantation does not require sutures, as the incision is self-sealing.

•You may experience some temporary discomfort, such as blurry vision, light sensitivity, and eye irritation. You will need to use antibiotic and steroid eye drops for several weeks to help prevent infection and inflammation. It is important to avoid rubbing your eyes and to wear protective eyewear during the healing process.
