About Us

Watany's History Vision & Mission Tribute
Hegaz Branch 2001

Since its establishment in 2001 by Dr. Riad Fikry, Dr. Fathy Fawzy, and Dr. Abdallah Hassouna, Al Watany Eye Hospital has taken solid steps toward establishing itself as one of the most trusted medical service providers in ophthalmology. Watany Eye Hospitals and Centers provide various treatment option in medical and surgical ophthalmology by a group of the most skilled and famous consultants in Egypt.

Thawra Branch 2015

Built on 2,336 m2 on the outskirts of Cairo and at the joint route between the city and its newest suburbs, our second premises boasts 10 operation rooms, femtosecond laser machines in 2 refractive suites, 1 femtosmile laser machine, 16 fully equipped examination rooms, and full investigative tools in a state-of-the-art eight-story building. In our dedication to excellence, we have successfully stepped into today’s high-tech culture by establishing a paperless operating system. Our homegrown electronic health records system encompasses outflow control across the hospital facilities and financial services as it integrates with clinical and affiliate remote service centers.

Hurghada Branch 2023

Watany Eye Hospitals and Centers did not ignore Hurghada, the city of magic and beauty.
Located on the southwestern coast of the Red Sea and one of Egypt’s most popular tourist destinations, the city of Hurghada has been gaining fame as a destination for medical tourism due to its low cost, highly skilled and experienced medical staff who speaks several languages, and wonderful atmosphere. Dr. Riad Fikry

Past Chairman of Watany Eye Hospitals

A tribute to a beloved mentor, a brilliant mind, a kind heart, and a true inspiration to us all.

• Dr. Riad Fikry, MD, was the chairman and medical director of Watany Eye Hospital in east Cairo, Egypt.

• His ambition was to establish a state-of-the-art hospital. He saw it as a market necessity and a place that would be a haven for both doctors and patients.

• In 2001, Dr. Riad founded Watany Eye Hospital with two other prominent ophthalmologists, Dr. Fathy Fawzy and Dr. Abdallah Hassouna.

• The hospital began its operation as a relatively small two-floor section of a building in a busy street in Cairo.

• Dr. Riad became chairman of the hospital and undertook numerous scientific activities in the hospital, insisted on strict subspecialty practice, an idea that was novel to the Egyptian market—and drew expertise from all over Cairo to develop and run the new model.

• He initiated the first ophthalmology residency program in a private hospital in Egypt and started an annual scientific meeting.

• Dr. Riad was the driving force behind the hospital becoming Egypt’s International Chapter Affiliate for the Association for Research in Vision and Ophthalmology (ARVO-Egypt), and chaired the fledgling chapter for 3 years.

• With him at the helm, the hospital’s reputation grew, and space became tight. Patients were coming from all over Egypt, and the hospital’s catchment area extended beyond Cairo to include Upper Egypt and the Nile Delta. Therefore, with the same partners, he proceeded to build the biggest standalone private ophthalmic hospital in Egypt.

• Standing on 4,000 m2 in the outskirts of Cairo, on the joint route between the city and its newest suburbs, and on the way to the Suez Canal cities, it is a memorial to him for those who witnessed his utter dedication to it.

• Dr. Riad Fikry test-drove the hospital but did not see it in full operation. He passed away while he and his son, Dr. Ramy Riad, were on their way to operate on a group of patients using femtosecond cataract, which Dr. Riad was introducing in Egypt. He was 63.

• There is not a day or a moment that we do not think of him and remember his advice and ideas. Dr. Riad has inspired many of his colleagues and juniors, leaving his mark on their careers. But his real talent was his personal approach—the way he talked to each member of Al Watany Eye Hospital family as an individual and addressed their issues privately, wisely, and, most important, tirelessly.

Dr. Riad Fikry was a truly special person. He touched the lives of so many people, and he will be deeply missed. But his legacy will live on in the hearts of all who knew him.

Thank you, Dr. Riad Fikry, for everything
