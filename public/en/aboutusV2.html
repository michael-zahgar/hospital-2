About Us

Watany's History Vision & Mission Tribute
Hegaz Branch 2001

Since its establishment in 2001 by Dr. Riad Fikry, Dr. Fathy Fawzy, and Dr. Abdallah Hassouna, Al Watany Eye Hospital has taken solid steps toward establishing itself as one of the most trusted medical service providers in ophthalmology. Watany Eye Hospitals and Centers provide various treatment options in medical and surgical ophthalmology by a group of the most skilled and renowned consultants in Egypt.

Thawra Branch 2015

Built on 2,336 m2 on the outskirts of Cairo and at the joint route between the city and its newest suburbs, our second premises boasts 10 operation rooms, 4refractive suites, 16 fully equipped examination rooms, and full investigative tools in a state-of-the-art eight-story building.
In our dedication to excellence, we have successfully stepped into today’s high-tech culture by establishing a paperless operating system. Our homegrown electronic health records system encompasses outflow control across the hospital facilities and financial services as it integrates with clinical and affiliate remote service centers.

Hurghada Branch 2023

The latest branch is located within walking distance of Hurghada Airport and all hotels and tourist resorts, making it convenient for both locals and visitors. In addition to its clinical services, Watany Eye Center in Hurghada has a dedicated team of medical tourism staff to help schedule appointments and coordinate travel and accommodation for international patients. Dr. Riad Fikry

(1951- 2014)

Co-Founder and Past Chairman of Watany Eye Hospitals

A tribute to a beloved mentor, an exceptional ophthalmologist, a unique father figure and a true inspiration

• Dr. Riad Fikry, MD, was the chairman and medical director of Watany Eye Hospital in east Cairo, Egypt since its establishment in 2001.

• His ambition was to establish a state-of-the-art hospital. He saw it as a market necessity and a haven for both doctors and patients.

• As the CEO and medical director of WEH, he introduced subspecialty practice in Ophthalmology- an idea that was novel to the Egyptian market at the time and hence drew expertise from all over Cairo to run and further develop the new model.

• Moreover, he initiated the first ophthalmology residency program in a private hospital in Egypt

• Dr. Riad was the driving force behind the hospital becoming Egypt’s International Chapter Affiliate for the Association for Research in Vision and Ophthalmology (ARVO-Egypt), and chaired the fledgling chapter for 3 years. With the team, he started an annual scientific meeting of ARVO-Egypt aimed at supporting local and regional research in vision sciences.

• With him at the helm, the practice grew and the catchment area extended beyond Cairo to include Upper Egypt and the Nile Delta. What started as a relatively small two-floor section of a building in a busy street in East Cairo proceeded to become the biggest standalone private ophthalmology hospital in Egypt and the Middle East.

• Dr. Riad Fikry test-drove the hospital but did not see it in full operation. He passed away peacefully while he and his son, Dr. Ramy Riad, were on their way to surgery. He was 63.

• There is not a day or a moment that we do not think of him and remember his advice and ideas. Dr. Riad has inspired many of his colleagues and juniors, leaving his mark on their careers. But his real talent was his personal approach—the way he individually communicated to the Watany Eye Hospital family members, addressing their issues privately, wisely, and, most important, tirelessly.

• Standing on 4,000 m2 in the outskirts of Cairo, WEH is a memorial to him and his utter dedication.

• Dr. Riad Fikry was a truly special person. He touched the lives of so many people, and he will be deeply missed. But his legacy will live on in the hearts of all who knew him.

Thank you, Dr. Riad Fikry, for everything.
